Request validation

The payloads are usually validated through the use of @Valid annotation with the respective model/payload objects expressing their validity (usually through the use of annotations). Operations failing the request validation will respond with a HTTP status code of 400, the failed payload and the reason(s) for failure after being handled by an exception handler that also logs the failure(s) to the log files.

Aspects and Advices

The operations are usually subject to being advised by custom Spring AOP advice classes. The aspects that are usually addressed include:

  • logging
  • profiling
  • exception handling (other than the validation errors)
